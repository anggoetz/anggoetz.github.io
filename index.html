Hello!

I’m a researcher, passionate about understanding how humans think about, and are impacted by, digital technologies.

Currently, I work at the Max Planck Institute for Software Systems, advised by Dr. Elissa M. Redmiles. I have led and assisted projects that investigate:

  • How digital content creators perform their labor, and how they think about the digital platforms and tools they use
  • How users feel about (harmful) ads they are exposed to on social media
  • Data privacy sentiment changes throughout the COVID-19 pandemic
  • TikTok usage metrics using user donated data

Previously, I worked as a market researcher based in Los Angeles, where I conducted qualitative interviews and basic quantitative analysis of survey data to help entertainment and tech companies understand their audiences.

I earned my BA in both Communication and Sociology from UCSB in 2019. I completed an honors thesis studying the relationship between human memory and the ability to save information on personal devices. I also aided my professors with projects on internet access and cognitive self-esteem, and personal closeness on social media.

I am currently based in Saarbrücken, where I am exploring German beer culture and taking advantage of Europe’s amazing railways and public transportation systems!
